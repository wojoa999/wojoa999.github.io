## Introduction
The introduction of AI in education has the potential to be an effective tool used to increase students' learning capabilities. The use of AI, personally, was limited due to the idea that I want to grasp the idea of learning through trial and error. Utilizing AI for every experience would limit my ability to learn effectively. Overusage would hinder my ability to learn and result in copying AI recommendations and solutions. AI was used once I became stuck and couldn’t proceed any further with my assignments. Gemini was the AI tool that I used and was found very useful in the times of need. The utilization of AI in the computer software course allowed me to maximize my learning potential.   
    
## Personal Experience with AI
Experience WODs: AI was slightly used to complete the experience WODs. With the tutorial videos, I didn’t need to utilize AI due to the ability to see where I was making mistakes. The experience WOD I did use AI was E61: experience testing. The commands that I was using in the terminal were throwing errors. I would prompt Gemini with “how do I fix [error message]”. AI was extremely helpful with finding out issues with the command errors. I was able to find that my MacBook software was dated and Gemini was able to find a solution to fix the error. 
In-class Practice WODs: AI was used to help me complete a few of the in-class practice WOD assignments. For Murphy's in-class assignment, Gemini was used to help with the formatting of the web design due to the “class” library having a large syntax. AI was prompted with “How do I shift [segment of code] to appear at the center on the navbar”. AI was found useful, it was able to provide me with a “use this class and this amount of padding” response.   
In-class WODs: Much like the in-class practice WODs, AI was used to help with the proper formatting of the webpage design. A particular in-class would that AI was used was the Maui Brewing Company WOD. Gemini was prompted “how do I format these images [corresponding lines of code] with the text appearing to the right of the image”. AI was fairly useful, I did take a few attempts to get the navbar to be formatted correctly, but AI was able to resolve the issue.  
Essays: AI was not used for the writing of essays for this course. The writing assignments were not difficult and mostly relied on personal reflection, so the use of AI was unnecessary. 
Final project: AI was used to help me complete the final project. I was tasked with setting up the database and the data storage format. I was having issues with deploying the database, so Gemini was prompted with “how do I fix [error message]”. AI was found to be useful in providing me with a list of possible solutions that I then would attempt to implement. Usually, the first couple of solutions would fix the issue that I was receiving. 
Learning a concept / tutorial: AI was not used to help learn a concept or tutorial. I found that the reading and the tutorial videos placed in each WOD were sufficient enough to properly learn the task.  
Answering a question in class or in Discord: AI was not used for answering a question in-class or on Discord. Most of the topics covered throughout the course were new for me, so I would rely on the at-home WODs to learn material. Throughout the semester, I don’t recall answering any question in-class or on Discord.  
Asking or answering a smart-question: AI was not utilized for asking or answering smart questions. I did not really find the need to ask any smart questions throughout the semester. I was able to find solutions through research or AI without using the smart questions section on Discord. 
Coding example: I didn’t use AI to provide me with any code examples. I never really thought about using AI to generate similar pieces of code. I usually prompted AI with a specific question to an issue I was receiving.
Explaining code: AI was not used for the explanation of code. I found the tutorial videos listed inside of each WOD informative enough to properly understand the code. The tutorials were efficient in helping in areas I wasn't familiar with. 
Writing code: AI was used to help me write code for the WODs. I used Gemini for fixing errors I didn't know how to resolve. I would prompt “how do I fix [segment of code] and this [error message]”. Gemini was great at resolving the errors that I was receiving, but occasionally would make mistakes. The VSCode feature GitHub Copilot was turned on, which allowed me to receive AI recommended code. GitHub Copilot didn’t need a prompt, but I found some of the recommendations not useful. 
Documenting code: For documenting code the use of AI was not implemented. I did not find myself documenting any code throughout the semester. Code documentation was not necessarily a requirement due to the athletic programming, but I do understand that it is a best practice for understanding code. 
Quality assurance: AI was implemented to provide quality assurance within my code. Typically, to fix ESLint errors I would utilize the quick fix feature of the GitHub Copilot for quick ESLint errors. This process did not require any prompts and fixed the errors such as spacing and new lines rather quickly. Gemini was prompted with “how do I fix [segment of code]’. I found this to be quite useful in finding and correcting my errors. 
Other uses in ICS 314 not listed: The EUT03 assignment wanted us to test the edge cases of LLM. It was required to use AI for this assignment. Gemini was prompted with “design a valid email address function with [requirements]”. Gemini was able to create the function for basic test cases, but lacked special character test cases. I did prove to be useful, but does have its limitations.
Impact on Learning and Understanding
The incorporation of AI allowed me to have a positive and productive learning experience. In turn, allowing me to enhance my understanding of software engineering concepts. AI was ultimately used where I found the tutorials lacking and couldn't figure out a solution based on the information provided. Occasionally, I would experience old tutorials that used different softwares that didn't match the current software being used. At times, I was completely new to some concepts and relied on a tutorial to help. I leaned into AI to help with understanding concepts, so I could work through what needed to be done. Gemini was a great tool for explaining the concepts behind what needed to be done and the concepts behind software engineering topics. Essentially, providing me with a solution and the reasoning behind it. I could dive deeper into the topics that I didn't fully understand to get more insight. AI allowed me to have a better understanding of topics and a positive outlook on the use of AI for this course.     

## Practical Applications 
The effectiveness of AI has the potential to enhance real-world solutions to computer engineering issues. AI has the effect of making knowledgeable personnel work more efficiently, rather than fixing an issue outright. As AI stands in the present, the code issued by AI isn't always the most efficient or best solution possible. Raising the question if AI is suitable to work independently for real-world activities. Google uses AI actively to debug and fix broken code within their software[1]. Decreasing the amount of time and waste it takes to resolve the issue. While AI is not actively writing Google’s code, AI is utilized to efficiently find issues and generate solutions. The programmer ultimately has the final confirmation to whether the code is suitable or not. The use of AI in practical applications can be useful to help boost the efficiency of software engineering solutions, but a knowledgeable programmer is still needed to ensure the AI output is sufficient.       
  
## Challenges and Opportunities
Potential challenges and limitations of AI that were observed in the course was the occasional loop that AI cycles in. For instance, when using pgAdmin I was having issues with seeding the database from the provided template. I resorted to asking AI what changes in the code must be adjusted to fix the error I was receiving. AI would make recommendations and I would make adjustments to the code and receive a different error, which I thought I was making progress. This pattern would continue until AI reached a point to where its recommendation was the original code I started with. Ultimately, I was able to correct the issue, but it took time prompting AI correctly to finally come up with a resolution. Another limitation was observed in the experience EUT03, where I was tasked with evaluating LLM code generating capabilities. AI was tasked with creating an email validation function for email addresses. Only the basic test cases passed the test cases for a valid email address. The tests that AI had missed were mostly the special characters that are used within an email address. After prompting AI to adjust to these cases, it was able to create a proper email validation function. Otherwise AI was still a great tool that was utilized for educational purposes, but possessed limitations in always functioning correctly.      

## Comparative Analysis
Traditional teaching methods and AI-enhanced learning, in the context of software engineering, have the potential to create an optimal learning environment. In the overall learning environment there are areas in which each learning method may not be fully suitable. Traditional teaching methods lean on engagement to supply educational material to students wanting to learn. The use of AI lacks in the engagement of the learning environment due to the prompting and receiving a solution. Creating an environment that students are relying on computer generated solutions, which may not be correct, to learn. Depending how AI is utilized will determine the knowledge retention of material. If students are taking notes when actively using AI, they may see a retention in the material that is actively used. AI just used for a solution may not actively promote any knowledge retention. In traditional learning methods students are constantly paying attention to material and actively taking notes. While you may not understand the material fully yet, the notes taken in-person are beneficial to the learning process. Traditional  and AI-enhanced learning methods promote the idea of skill development. In the traditional setting, homework assignments and exam reviews promote skill development throughout the semester. AI can be used to generate additional practice that is similar to the coursework. These two learning methods combined allow students to optimally enhance their skills within computer engineering. Neither traditional or AI-enhanced methods are perfect for ensuring you have properly learned all the material, together you can increase your knowledge by combining the learning methods.         

## Future Considerations 
The future role of AI in software engineering education has the potential to boost the effectiveness of education, but possess the power to diminish learning. Prompting AI doesn’t always produce the proper outcome the user is looking for or even the same solutions. With the pace AI is evolving, the downfalls of AI may, in the case of inaccuracy, may be fully resolved. AI could potentially be a great learning tool integrated in education. Providing students and novices who wish to gain better insights on learning. Effectively increasing the efficiency that future students can work and learn. This potentially places education at risk due to the ability to receive a quick solution to the user. Rather than learning necessary topics, students have the potential to prompt AI just to complete assignments and tasks. Overusing AI defeats the point of learning. The evolution of AI in educational material must be tread carefully, so that the purpose of education and learning is retained. The connection between learning and the use of AI will need to be determined to ensure the proper education criteria is met.       

## Conclusion
The use of AI in the software engineering course allowed for a better understanding of material that I either was struggling with or didn't know how to complete. By leveraging the experience tutorials I was able to grasp a foundation for the assignments I was tasked with completing. When the tutorial explanations were not adequate, utilizing AI helped guide me through topics I didn't understand. Although AI wasn't completely perfect in finding the correct solutions, it still proved to be a great tool to use throughout the semester. The course allowed us to test the limitations of AI by designing an email address template and testing if the email address was valid. Combining traditional and AI-enhanced learning methods would optimize the learning capabilities of students within the software engineering course. Overall, the usage of AI throughout the semester provided insight into new topics without hindering the learning capability.
